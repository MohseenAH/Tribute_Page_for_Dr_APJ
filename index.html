About Dr. Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 - 27 July 2015) was a revered aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Widely known as the "People's President," he was beloved for his humility, wisdom, and unwavering dedication to inspiring the nation's youth.

Rising from humble beginnings, Dr. Kalam became a central figure in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the title of "Missile Man of India". He was instrumental in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

After his presidency, he returned to his passion for teaching, writing, and public service, dedicating the rest of his life to interacting with students and igniting young minds.

Key Highlights of His Life:

  • Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • Served as the 11th President of India and was a recipient of the Bharat Ratna (1997).
  • Project Director for India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • Led the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, developing missiles like Agni and Prithvi.
  • Authored several inspirational books, including his autobiography "Wings of Fire."

Dr. Kalam passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong in 2015, doing what he loved most. His life and work continue to be a source of immense inspiration for millions across the world.
